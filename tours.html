7-Day Tour

Seven Churches of Revelation & Istanbul

A day-by-day look at your journey through Turkey's most sacred and historic sites.

1

Arrive in Istanbul

Transfer to your hotel in Istanbul — originally known as Byzantium, then Constantinople. A fascinating city situated between two continents, Europe and Asia, where centuries of history await.

2

Istanbul — Hagia Sophia, Blue Mosque & Grand Bazaar

Today we visit Hagia Sophia, the Hippodrome and obelisk brought from Egypt, then the Blue Mosque and the Basilica Cistern. We enjoy lunch and free time in the Grand Bazaar, where the air is filled with the enticing aromas of cinnamon, caraway, saffron, mint, thyme and every other conceivable herb and spice.

3

Gallipoli — Dardanelles — Troy — Çanakkale

Drive to Gallipoli, crossing the Dardanelles Strait by ferryboat. Visit ancient Troy and see the replica of the wooden horse. Then continue to Çanakkale for overnight.

4

Pergamum — Thyatira — Izmir (Smyrna)

First visit to Pergamum with its impressive sites including the Altar of Zeus, referred to as the "Throne of Satan" in the Book of Revelation. We continue on to Thyatira, famous for its purple dyes, before returning to the hotel. Enjoy a panoramic drive of the city of Smyrna before checking into the hotel overnight in Izmir.

Revelation 2:12-13 — "To the angel of the church in Pergamum write... I know where you dwell — where Satan's throne is."
5

Sardis — Philadelphia — Laodicea — Hierapolis (Pamukkale)

Early morning start visiting Sardis, one of the most picturesque sites of the Seven Churches. In Philadelphia we see the remains of a Byzantine Basilica and 11th-century frescoes. Then on to Laodicea before reaching Hierapolis, where ancient hot springs cascade over steep cliffs forming stalactites and pools.

Revelation 3:7-8 — "To the angel of the church in Philadelphia... See, I have placed before you an open door that no one can shut."
6

Ephesus — Kuşadası

Today enjoy a visit to the site of Ephesus, which includes the Artemission Temple — one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World — as well as the Basilica of St. John and the Ephesus archaeological museum. Overnight in Kuşadası.

Acts 18:19-21 — Paul entered the synagogue at Ephesus and reasoned with the Jews, planting the seeds of one of the most important early churches.
7

Departure

Fly from Izmir to Istanbul to connect with our flight home. We hope that the memories of your visit will accompany you whenever you travel through the pages of Revelation, reading about the Seven Churches — and that you will choose to come with us on your next Bible land journey.

8-Day Tour

Istanbul & Seven Churches of Asia Minor

An extended experience with two full days in Istanbul, a Bosphorus cruise, and deeper exploration of all seven churches.

1

Arrival to Istanbul

Welcome to Turkey, the crossroads between East and West! Our representative will meet you at Istanbul airport and escort you on a private transfer to the hotel. Enjoy the rest of the day at your own leisure to explore the hidden treasures of this magnificent city.

2

Full Day Istanbul Tour

Our journey begins in Istanbul — the only city in the world situated on two continents. We visit the Topkapi Palace, Hagia Sophia, the Blue Mosque and Hippodrome where chariot races and the Byzantine riots took place. Visit the Spice Bazaar where the air is filled with the enticing aromas of cinnamon, caraway, saffron, mint, thyme and every other conceivable herb and spice.

3

Full Day Istanbul — Flight to Izmir

Today's visits begin with the Grand Bazaar — one of the largest and oldest covered markets in the world, with 61 covered streets and over 3,000 shops! Later, transfer to the port for a cruise on the Bosphorus — a traditional excursion by boat along the waterway separating Europe and Asia, lined with old wooden villas, marble palaces, fortresses, and small fishing villages. Afternoon visit to the Underground Cistern and transfer to Istanbul airport for flight to Izmir.

4

Pergamum — Thyatira — Izmir

Drive to Pergamum, modern-day Bergama. Explore the impressive Acropolis, the steepest theater in the world and the Asklepion. See the Altar of Zeus mentioned as the "Throne of Satan" in the Book of Revelation. Continue to Thyatira and visit the remains of the Basilica. Enjoy a panoramic drive through Smyrna, one of the Seven Churches of Asia.

Revelation 2:12-13 — "To the angel of the church in Pergamum write... I know where you dwell — where Satan's throne is."
5

Sardis — Philadelphia — Laodicea — Hierapolis

Early morning start visiting Sardis, one of the most picturesque sites of the Seven Churches. We visit the great Temple of Artemis and the Temple of Cybele. In Philadelphia we see the remains of the Byzantine Basilica and 11th-century frescoes. Proceed to Laodicea where we see the ancient theater and the Gate of Domitian. Today's tour ends at Hierapolis (Pamukkale) where ancient hot springs cascade over steep cliffs.

Revelation 3:15-16 — "I know your deeds, that you are neither cold nor hot... So, because you are lukewarm, I am about to spit you out of my mouth." (To the church at Laodicea)
6

Ephesus — Kuşadası

Drive to Ephesus and visit the ancient city and the Basilica of St. John. The Apostle Paul preached to the Ephesians and lived here for three years. We tour the spectacular ruins where excavations still continue in this once great city, founded in the 10th century BC. Visit the House of the Virgin Mary before driving to Kuşadası for dinner and overnight.

Revelation 2:4-5 — "Yet I hold this against you: You have forsaken the love you had at first. Consider how far you have fallen! Repent and do the things you did at first."
7

Izmir — Istanbul

In the morning we transfer to Izmir airport for an afternoon flight to Istanbul. The remainder of the day is free to explore the city on your own. Discover the old and new parts of Istanbul and get a real feel for this fascinating city!

8

Departure

Meet with your escort and driver for transfer to the airport for your international flight. We hope that visiting the Churches found in Revelation has brought you a new understanding of the Bible to go along with fresh memories, souvenirs, and a renewed interest in Turkey — the land of culture, faith, and mystery.
